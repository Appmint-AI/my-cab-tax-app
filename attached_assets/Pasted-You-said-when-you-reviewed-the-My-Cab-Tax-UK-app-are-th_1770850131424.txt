In reviewing the My Cab Tax UK app, there are several "high-utility" features that we should definitely migrate to the USA version. While the tax codes differ, the workflow of a driver is universal.

Here are the top UK features to adapt for the My Cab Tax USA build on Replit/Cloud Run:

1. The "Rolling Tax" Estimated Liability

The UK version has a "Real-Time Tax" figure. In the USA, self-employed drivers often get hit with a surprise 15.3% Self-Employment tax plus Income Tax.

The Feature: A dashboard widget that calculates your Estimated Quarterly Tax Payment.

USA Adaptation: Calculate (NetIncome×15.3%) plus a simplified estimate for Federal Income Tax. This helps drivers avoid the IRS "Underpayment Penalty."

2. Automated "Platform Fee" Reconciliation

UK apps often handle Uber/Bolt "Commission" deductions automatically. In the USA, a common mistake is only reporting the "payout" rather than the "gross fare."

The Feature: A 1099-K / 1099-NEC "Gross-Up" tool.

USA Adaptation: A feature where drivers can enter their weekly Uber/Lyft summary, and the app automatically splits it into Gross Income (Line 1 of Schedule C) and Commissions/Fees (Line 10).

3. Biometric "Quick-Log"

The UK app allows FaceID/Fingerprint login for fast entry between fares.

The Feature: Auth0-supported Biometric Login.

USA Adaptation: Since we are using Auth0, enable Biometrics so a driver can log a $5 car wash or a $60 gas fill-up in 10 seconds while at the pump, without typing a long password.

4. "Audit-Safe" Receipt Scanner

UK drivers use this to comply with "Making Tax Digital" (MTD). In the USA, the IRS requires receipts for any expense over $75 (but it's best practice to have them for everything).

The Feature: Camera-to-Cloud Receipt Storage.

USA Adaptation: When a user takes a photo, the app should automatically extract the Date, Amount, and Vendor using OCR, then store the image in the Google Cloud "Tax Vault."

5. Multi-Car Management

Many UK taxi firms manage small fleets. In the USA, many drivers switch between a "gas saver" for weekdays and a "premium vehicle" for weekends.

The Feature: Multi-Vehicle Logbooks.

USA Adaptation: Allow the driver to create "Vehicle Profiles." The IRS requires separate records for each vehicle if you use the "Actual Expenses" method for one and "Standard Mileage" for the other.
