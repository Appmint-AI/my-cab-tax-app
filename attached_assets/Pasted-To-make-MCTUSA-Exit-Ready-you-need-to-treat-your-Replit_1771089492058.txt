To make MCTUSA "Exit-Ready," you need to treat your Replit Agent like a senior engineer writing a legacy for their successor.

1. The "Architectural Blueprint" (The PLAN.md)

Acquirers look for a clear design philosophy. If they can't find a map of how the data flows, they assume it’s a mess.

Tell the Replit Agent:

"Create a PLAN.md file in the root directory. This file must explain:

The Core Tech Stack: Why we chose Replit, Stripe, and your specific DB.

The 'Sovereignty' Logic: Explain the technical choice to keep 1099-K math local vs. server-side.

Data Flow: A text-based diagram showing how a receipt goes from the camera → AI Scanner → Vault → Tax Form."

2. High-Value Commentary (Self-Documenting Code)

Standard comments tell you what the code is doing. Acquisition-ready comments tell you why it’s doing it.

Tell the Replit Agent:

"Apply JSDoc/Docstring standards to every function. For the complex 1099-K matching and Gross-Up logic, add a 'Compliance Note' in the comments:

// Compliance Note: This logic follows IRS Rev. Proc. 2026-XX regarding 1099-K reconciliation.

// Why: We use a hash-based immutable storage to ensure audit-trail integrity for M&A due diligence."

3. The "IP Cleanliness" Audit

Big players are terrified of legal "taint" (e.g., using code that wasn't properly licensed).

Tell the Replit Agent:

"Create a LICENSES.md file.

List every single 3rd-party library (NPM packages, APIs) we are using.

Confirm all are under MIT or Apache 2.0 licenses.

Crucial: Add a section stating that 100% of the custom logic was generated and owned by the company founder to ensure a 'Clean IP' chain of custody."

4. The "Unit Test" Proof of Success

A buyer won't believe your app works just because you say it does. They want to see automated tests passing.

Tell the Replit Agent:

"Build a /tests suite for the Tax Engine.

Create 10 'Edge Case' scenarios (e.g., a driver with 5 states, or a driver with $0 income but $5,000 in mileage).

If I run npm test, it should show 10/10 passing. This proves to a future buyer that our math is mathematically sound and stable."
