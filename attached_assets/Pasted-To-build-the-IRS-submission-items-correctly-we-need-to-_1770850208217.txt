To build the IRS submission items correctly, we need to implement the exact math and logic the IRS uses to calculate "taxable profit" and "estimated tax."

Since it is now 2026, we must use the updated tax figures (such as the 72.5 cents per mile rate) to ensure our users get the maximum legal deduction.

1. The IRS "Self-Employment Tax" Logic
Self-employment tax isn't just a flat percentage of total income; it applies to 92.35% of the driver's net profit.

The Math for the Replit Agent:

"Add a calculateTax() function to the Submission module. It must follow this logic:

Net Profit: Gross Income (from 1099-K/NEC) minus Total Business Expenses.

Taxable Base: Net Profit × 0.9235.

SE Tax Owed: Taxable Base × 0.153 (15.3%).

Taxable Income Adjustment: Subtract 50% of the SE Tax from the Net Profit before calculating Federal Income Tax."

2. Updated 2026 Mileage Logic
The IRS just increased the rate. If we use the old 2024/2025 rates, we are literally losing money for our drivers.

Tell the Replit Agent:

"Set the default 2026 Business Mileage Rate to 72.5 cents per mile. Create a 'Mileage Methodology' toggle:

Standard Mileage: Miles × 0.725.

Actual Expenses: Sum of Gas + Repairs + Insurance + Depreciation.

Validation: If 'Standard Mileage' is chosen, the app should hide the 'Gas' and 'Repair' categories from the total deduction to prevent 'double-dipping,' which is a major IRS audit red flag."

3. The "1099-K Gross-Up" Module
This is the most confusing part for drivers. Uber/Lyft report the Gross Fare (what the rider paid) on the 1099-K, even though the driver never saw the full amount.

Tell the Replit Agent:

"Create a 1099-K Entry Form. It should have three fields:

Gross Amount (Box 1a): This goes straight to Schedule C, Line 1.

Platform Fees (Uber/Lyft Commission): The app must automatically subtract this as an expense (Schedule C, Line 10).

Net Payout: A calculated field to help the driver verify the numbers against their bank statements."

4. Submission Checklist (The "IRS-Ready" Pack)
When the driver clicks "Export," the app should generate a summary that mirrors the Schedule C form perfectly.

App Category	Schedule C Line	Driver Action
Gross Fares	Line 1	Input from 1099-K.
Standard Mileage	Line 9	Automated from Mileage Tracker.
Commissions/Fees	Line 10	Input from Platform Summaries.
Supplies	Line 22	Receipts for water, phone mounts, etc.
Estimated Tax	Form 1040-ES	Our "Quarterly Estimate" calculation.
Strategic Build Order:

We should build the 1099-K Gross-Up tool first because that is the foundation of their income. Then, we build the Mileage Log (using the 0.725 rate) as their primary deduction.
